Masks that Matter

Sabreen and Aarna – classmates and neighbors, studying in grade 6 at Oakridge International School, Mohali learnt about how people are facing difficulties to get face masks for themselves due to the shortage in supply. They were particularly worried about the homeless and poor, who could not afford such high-priced masks.While Aarna and Sabreen knew how to make masks but making in bulk quantities was a challenge. Guided by Sabreen’s mother who works as the Senior Nursing Officer at PGIMER Chandigarh , the duo hired a tailor to begin stitching these masks together. Once the first lot of masks were delivered , the kids sanitized these masks at their home and started distributing it to the needy through the help of other volunteers. At their first attempt, each one of them donated 200 masks and another 200 are in process. In the future, these brave little kids want to continue to serve the community, be it through making and donating masks or in some other way.

what's new

  • All possible safety measures against Covid-19

    While in the lockdown for pandemic situation, Covid-19, Oakridge schools have been upgrading their campuses. They are taking all possible security measures to prevent it from infecting any student, teacher or any of the staff. The staff have been trained to learn how to santitize the rooms, clean the buses, sweep the gardens and so on. They have taken all possible measures that they could take, from replacing normal taps and sanitizers to automatic ones, to measuring distancing between desks and taping red crosses on the seats which did not maintain distancing. All the activity class rooms were expanded and webcams were placed in every class, so that children in the online batch (since they cannot let everybody in at once) don't miss out on what the physical school batch are doing.


  • New learnings, New tools

    In Covid-19 lockdown, our teachers know the old board and marker won't do the trick. They alsoo know that google meet, zoom and other places are not as safe secure and easy to use Microsoft Teams is. The school has given us E-mail addresses which only other Oakridge E-mail addresses can contact, keeping us safe and secure. They have created MS Teams accounts for us using those E-mails so the easy to use MS Teams is also secure. Our teachers have been provided with multiple different tools like Microsoft whiteboard, Classroomscreen, GoNoodle and Bitmoji that help them teach. All of them have purposes, and even though everything has to be online now, our schooling has not lost its flair of ma'am presenting her whiteboard (offline or online), we all also wanting to present (our screen or write on the board), and such other habits.
